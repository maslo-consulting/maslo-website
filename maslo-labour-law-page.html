01

Code on Wages, 2019

REPLACES: Minimum Wages Act, Payment of Wages Act, Equal Remuneration Act, Payment of Bonus Act

Redefines wages — basic pay must be at least 50% of total CTC. Directly increases PF, gratuity, and bonus calculations. Most companies need CTC restructuring to remain compliant.

Wage restructuringBonus recalculationEqual pay
02

Code on Social Security, 2020

REPLACES: EPF Act, ESI Act, Gratuity Act, Maternity Benefit Act + 5 others

Extends social security to gig workers and platform employees for the first time. Changes gratuity eligibility and introduces portable benefit accounts. Major impact on contract and freelance workforce management.

PF & ESI changesGig worker coverageGratuity revision
03

Code on Industrial Relations, 2020

REPLACES: Industrial Disputes Act, Trade Unions Act, Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act

Raises retrenchment and closure permission threshold from 100 to 300 workers. Introduces fixed-term employment as mainstream. Major implications for hire-and-fire policies and standing orders.

Retrenchment rulesFixed-term employmentStanding orders
04

OSH & Working Conditions Code, 2020

REPLACES: Factories Act, Contract Labour Act, Mines Act + 10 others

Changes working hour limits, overtime rules, and leave entitlements. Introduces 4-day work week option. Sets new standards for contract labour management and welfare obligations across all establishments.

Working hoursContract labourLeave entitlements

Frequently asked questions
What companies ask us about labour law compliance
Clear answers to the questions founders and HR heads ask most.

Have India's new labour codes come into effect yet?

The four new labour codes have been passed by Parliament and several states have already notified their rules and begun implementation. Central enforcement is imminent. Companies that wait for full enforcement will have zero preparation time. The smart move is to use Compliance360's New Labour Code Readiness Dashboard now to understand your current code-wise compliance percentage and act on the smart roadmap before enforcement catches up.

How do the new labour codes change salary and CTC structures?

The Code on Wages redefines wages to mean at least 50% of total CTC. Companies that have loaded CTC with allowances to reduce PF liability must restructure. This directly increases PF contributions, gratuity liability, and bonus calculations. Compliance360's audit engine identifies this gap automatically, quantifies the financial impact, and the smart roadmap converts it into a step-by-step CTC restructuring plan with timelines.

What is Professional Tax and Labour Welfare Fund — why are they state-specific?

Professional Tax (PT) and Labour Welfare Fund (LWF) are levied by state governments — not the central government — so their rates, slabs, due dates, and applicability vary significantly from state to state. Some states have PT, others do not. LWF contribution frequency and amounts differ by state. Compliance360 maps your state-wise presence and tracks PT and LWF obligations separately for each state, ensuring you never miss a state-specific deadline.

How is Compliance360 different from a compliance calendar or checklist?

A compliance calendar tells you when something is due. A checklist tells you what to check. Compliance360 audits whether you are actually compliant across 18+ statutory categories, scores your readiness for each of the 4 new labour codes, converts every gap into a prioritised roadmap item, tracks ongoing obligations automatically, manages holiday calendars using AI, and produces exportable reports for management and regulators. It is the difference between a reminder app and a full compliance management system — built specifically for Indian labour law.

What exportable reports does Compliance360 produce?

Compliance360 generates audit reports in both PDF and Excel formats — suitable for board presentations, management reviews, and regulatory submissions. Reports include overall compliance scores by category, code-wise readiness percentages for all four new labour codes, gap summaries with risk ratings, the smart roadmap action plan, and compliance tracker status. All reports are structured to meet the documentation requirements of labour inspectors and auditors.

Single-site employers get simplicity. Multi-state enterprises get unified visibility across all locations from one dashboard.
